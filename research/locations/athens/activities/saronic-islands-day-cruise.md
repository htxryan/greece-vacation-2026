# Saronic Islands Day Cruise

Escape the bustling streets of Athens for a day and sail across the sparkling Saronic Gulf to visit three of Greece's most charming islands: Hydra, Poros, and Aegina. This full-day cruise is one of the most popular day trips from Athens, offering a taste of Greek island life without the commitment of overnight travel or ferry logistics.

The journey departs from Marina Kallitheas (just south of central Athens) early in the morning and returns by early evening. Along the way, you'll enjoy a buffet lunch on board, live Greek music and dancing on the return journey, and free time to explore each island's unique character. Hydra enchants with its car-free cobblestone streets and artistic heritage; Poros delights with its clock tower views and pine-covered hills; and Aegina satisfies with its famous pistachios and ancient Temple of Aphaia.

For October travelers, this cruise offers ideal conditions: pleasant weather without summer's crowds, warm enough for outdoor deck lounging (though swimming may be refreshing rather than warm), and the islands operating at a relaxed, authentic pace.

## Details

### Timing & Duration

- **Departure**: 7:45 AM from Marina Kallitheas (arrive 30 minutes early)
- **Return**: 7:45 PM (approximately 12 hours total)
- **Best Day**: Any day of the week; cruises run daily year-round

*Note: Timings may vary slightly due to weather or port conditions.*

### What to Expect at Each Island

**Hydra (1 hour 40 minutes)**
- Famous car-free island where donkeys are the only transport
- Wander the charming harbor lined with cafes and art galleries
- Leonard Cohen once lived here; find the street named after him
- Flat waterfront walk is easy; cobblestone streets up the hill are steeper
- Perfect for: Photography, people-watching, café stop

**Poros (50 minutes)**
- Climb 175 steps to the iconic Clock Tower for panoramic views
- Or stay at the waterfront for coffee and souvenir browsing
- Pine-covered island with neoclassical architecture
- Perfect for: Quick exploration, photography, waterfront relaxing

**Aegina (2 hours)**
- The largest island with the most time to explore
- Famous for Protected Designation of Origin pistachios—buy fresh or as ice cream
- Temple of Aphaia (8 miles from port, €35 taxi round-trip) is one of Greece's best-preserved ancient temples
- Waterfront tavernas perfect for a pre-departure snack
- Perfect for: Food lovers, ancient history buffs, souvenir shopping

### Onboard Experience

- **Buffet Lunch**: Served while sailing, includes variety of Greek and international dishes
- **Bars**: Multiple bars including sun deck bar (drinks not included in standard ticket)
- **Entertainment**: Live Greek music and folk dancing show on return journey
- **Facilities**: Indoor and outdoor lounges, boutique shopping, sun deck
- **Atmosphere**: Festive but not rowdy; family-friendly

### October Considerations

- **Weather**: Expect pleasant 22°C (72°F) days, but bring a light jacket for sea breezes
- **Swimming**: Water temperature is cooler in October (around 21°C/70°F)—refreshing but not warm. Some cruises offer swimming stops, but this isn't the main focus
- **Crowds**: Significantly fewer tourists than summer; islands feel more authentic
- **Availability**: Cruises run daily, but booking 2-3 days ahead recommended

### What to Bring

- Comfortable walking shoes (cobblestones on all islands)
- Light jacket or sweater for sea breeze and air-conditioning
- Sunscreen and sunglasses (October sun is still strong)
- Camera for photography opportunities
- Cash for island purchases (€30-50 per person recommended)
- Modest clothing if visiting churches or religious sites

### Accessibility Notes

- **Ships**: The cruise ships (Cosmos and Platytera Ton Ouranon) welcome wheelchair users, but advance notice is required. Contact reservations directly.
- **Companions**: Passengers requiring assistance should travel with a companion
- **Hydra Challenge**: Hydra's cobblestone streets and steps may be challenging for those with mobility issues. The flat waterfront area is accessible.
- **Elderly-Friendly**: Ample seating on board; waterfront areas on each island are generally flat

## Logistics

### Departure Point

**Marina Kallitheas** (not Piraeus main port)
- Address: 155 Syggrou Avenue, 17121 Nea Smyrni, Athens
- [View on Google Maps](https://www.google.com/maps/search/Marina+Kallitheas+Athens)

### Getting There from Central Athens

- **Taxi**: 15-20 minutes from Plaka/Syntagma (approximately €10-15)
- **Hotel Transfer**: Available as add-on for ~€20-30 per person
- **Public Transport**: Tram Line 3 to Marina Falirou, then 10-minute walk
