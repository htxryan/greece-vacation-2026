# National Archaeological Museum

The National Archaeological Museum of Athens is the largest museum in Greece and one of the most important archaeological museums in the world. Founded in 1893 and housed in a magnificent neoclassical building, it contains over 11,000 exhibits spanning from early prehistory (6th millennium BC) to late antiquity (5th century AD). This is the definitive collection of Greek antiquity artifacts worldwide—if you want to understand ancient Greek civilization in one place, this is it.

The museum's treasures include some of the most iconic artifacts ever discovered: the legendary Mask of Agamemnon from Mycenae, the Antikythera Mechanism (often called the world's first analog computer), stunning bronze statues recovered from ancient shipwrecks, and the beautifully preserved Akrotiri frescoes from Santorini's volcanic eruption. The collection is meticulously organized by era and artifact type across 8,000 square meters, making it accessible even for those new to ancient history.

For October visitors, the museum offers an air-conditioned respite from afternoon warmth (though October temperatures are pleasant), and the shoulder-season timing means fewer crowds than summer months. This is a history museum rather than an art museum—the focus is on understanding how ancient Greeks lived, worshipped, and created—making it an excellent match for the group's stated preference for history over art.

## Details

### Duration & Best Timing

- **Full experience**: 3-4 hours to explore all major collections with audio guide
- **Highlights tour**: 1-1.5 hours focusing on must-see pieces
- **Best time to visit**: Arrive early morning (8 AM opening) or after 1 PM on Tuesdays to avoid tour group crowds
- **Pro tip**: Start on the second floor to avoid the bottleneck at the main entrance

### What to Expect

The museum is organized into five major permanent collections:

1. **Collection of Prehistoric Antiquities** (6th millennium - 1050 BC)
   - Cycladic marble figurines that inspired modern artists like Henry Moore
   - Mycenaean gold treasures including the Mask of Agamemnon

2. **Sculpture Collection** (700 BC - 5th century AD)
   - Evolution of Greek sculpture over 1,200 years
   - Bronze Zeus/Poseidon from Cape Artemision
   - Jockey of Artemision

3. **Vase & Minor Arts Collection**
   - Ancient Greek pottery telling mythological stories
   - Jewelry and everyday objects

4. **Akrotiri Frescoes**
   - Vivid wall paintings from Bronze Age Santorini, preserved in volcanic ash

5. **Egyptian Collection**
   - Mummies, painted coffins, and statues—one of the most significant Egyptian collections in Southern Europe

### Must-See Highlights

- **Mask of Agamemnon**: Gold funerary mask from 16th century BC, the museum's most famous piece
- **Antikythera Mechanism**: 2nd-century BC astronomical device, the world's first analog computer
- **Bronze Poseidon/Zeus**: Dramatic 5th-century BC statue recovered from the sea
- **Jockey of Artemision**: Remarkable bronze of a child riding a horse
- **Cycladic Figurines**: Mysterious 3rd millennium BC marble figures

**Accessibility:**
- Wheelchair entrance on Vas. Herakleiou Street (side of building)
