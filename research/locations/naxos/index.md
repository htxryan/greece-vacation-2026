# Naxos

Naxos is the largest and greenest island in the Cyclades, located in the heart of the Aegean Sea. Known as the agricultural powerhouse of the island group, Naxos offers incredible farm-to-table dining, beautiful beaches, ancient ruins, and charming mountain villages—all without the crowds and premium prices of Santorini or Mykonos. The island combines authentic Greek island culture with stunning natural beauty and a rich mythology (legend says Zeus himself was raised here).

## Who This is For

**Ryan and Toni** will particularly love Naxos for its exceptional dining scene. The island grows almost all of its food locally, offering both fine dining experiences (like Axiotissa and Typografio) and authentic local tavernas (like Vassilis Tavern and To Gefyraki) that serve traditional recipes with ingredients grown within kilometers of the restaurant. The photogenic Portara at sunset and picturesque villages like Halki and Apiranthos provide endless photography opportunities.

**Andy and Kathy** will appreciate the relaxed pace, beautiful beaches with easy access (Agios Georgios is right in town), and the opportunity to enjoy good views and people-watching from the charming Naxos Town waterfront. The island is well-connected by ferry to other Cycladic islands if they want to explore further.

**All couples** will enjoy the excellent food scene, history museums (Archaeological Museum in Naxos Town), beautiful nature with minimal hiking required, unique local shopping (kitron liqueur, local cheeses, handcrafts), and the generally relaxed atmosphere.

## Reasons to Choose

- **Authentic Greek Experience**: Unlike the more tourist-focused Santorini and Mykonos, Naxos maintains a genuine, lived-in feel with real village life
- **Outstanding Food**: Farm-to-table is the norm here; the island produces its own potatoes (famous throughout Greece), cheeses, olive oil, meat, and the unique kitron liqueur
- **Best Beaches in the Cyclades**: Miles of golden sand beaches like Plaka, Agios Prokopios, and Agia Anna—less crowded than neighboring islands
- **Value for Money**: Lower prices than other popular Cycladic islands while offering comparable or better quality
- **Perfect October Weather**: September-October is considered ideal for visiting—warm swimming water (21°C/70°F), pleasant temperatures (17-23°C/63-73°F), fewer crowds, and grape/olive harvest season
- **Excellent Base Location**: Centrally located in the Cyclades with easy ferry connections to Santorini, Mykonos, Paros, and other islands

## Top Activities

- **The Portara (Temple of Apollo)**: Iconic marble gateway from 530 BC—spectacular at sunset
- **Old Town (Kastro)**: Wander the medieval Venetian castle quarter with its narrow alleys, shops, and cafes
- **Beach Days**: Relax at Agios Prokopios, Agia Anna, or the endless Plaka Beach
- **Village Exploration**: Visit Halki (former capital, kitron distillery tours) and Apiranthos (traditional mountain village)
- **Temple of Demeter**: Well-preserved 6th century BC temple outside town
- **Food & Drink**: Kitron distillery tours, local taverna dining, cheese tastings
- **Rina Cave**: Beautiful sea cave accessible by boat tour, ideal for swimming

## Details

### Suggested Stay Duration

3-5 days is ideal. Three days covers the main highlights (Portara, Old Town, beaches, a village or two), while 4-5 days allows for a more relaxed pace with time to explore multiple beaches, take day trips, and truly experience the local dining scene.

### Where to Stay

**Naxos Town (Chora)** or adjacent **Agios Georgios Beach** is recommended. This gives easy access to restaurants, the Old Town, ferry port, and a good beach—all walkable without needing a car.

### Getting Around

A rental car is recommended if you want to explore villages and remote beaches. However, the main beaches and town are accessible on foot or by local bus, so a car isn't strictly necessary for a more relaxed trip.

### October Considerations

- Weather is pleasant (17-23°C) with occasional brief showers
- Sea is still warm enough for swimming (21°C)
- Some beach establishments start closing after October 4th
- Naxos Town area remains fully operational
- Harvest season adds cultural interest (grapes, olives)
- Pack layers—warm days but cooler evenings

### Dining Highlights

- **Typografio**: Modern Greek in a former newspaper press at the castle base, rooftop views
- **Axiotissa**: Fine modern Greek and Naxian cuisine
- **To Gefyraki**: Hidden local taverna near Danakos with home-cooked Naxian recipes
- **Vassilis Tavern**: Family taverna in Melanes using their own farm produce
- **O Galanis**: Local favorite in Halki village
- **To Souvlaki tou Maki**: Best souvlaki with famous Naxos potatoes

## Logistics

### Getting There

**From Athens (Piraeus Port):**
- High-speed ferry: 3.5-4 hours (SeaJets, Hellenic Seaways)
- Conventional ferry: 5-5.5 hours (Blue Star Ferries)
- Multiple daily departures, prices €22-€116 depending on speed and class

**From Other Islands:**
- Santorini: ~2 hours by ferry
- Mykonos: ~1-2 hours by ferry
- Paros: ~30-45 minutes by ferry

**By Air:**
- Naxos has a small airport with connections from Athens (seasonal)
